28/**
29 * @ingroup logging_utils
30 * @brief Convert a logging level to its corresponding text style with color
31 *
32 * Maps each logging level to a specific color scheme using fmtlib's text_style:
33 * - Ignore: No styling (empty text_style)
34 * - Trace3, Trace2, Trace1, Trace: Gray foreground
35 * - Debug: Cyan foreground
36 * - Info: Green foreground
37 * - Warn: Yellow foreground
38 * - Error: Red foreground
39 * - Fatal: White foreground on red background
40 *
41 * @param level The logging level to convert
42 * @return fmt::text_style with appropriate foreground/background colors
43 *
44 * @note This function is only available when both __LOGENIUM_LOGGING_USE_COLOR_LOGS__
45 * and __LOGENIUM_LOGGING_USE_FMTLIB__ are defined
46 * @note The color scheme is designed to be visible on both light and dark terminals
47 */
48fmt::text_style ToColor(Level level);
